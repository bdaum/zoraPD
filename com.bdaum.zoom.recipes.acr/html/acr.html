Adobe Camera Raw and Lightroom Support

ZoRa Photo Director is able to reflect most of the development settings for RAW and DNG files made with Adobe Camera Raw or with Adobe Lightroom.

These settings (recipes) are stored either in an external XMP file located in the same folder as the original image (sidecar) or are embedded in the image file (as in the case of non-write-protected DNG files). ZoRa Photo Director is able to read these development recipes and to apply them when importing RAW or DNG files, refreshing or viewing them, or when deriving images from them during export operations.

To enable this support go the Window>Preferences>Import>Raw Conversion* and check the Adobe Camera Raw / Lightroom recipe detector. Make also sure that recipe detection is enabled (Process recipes when importing RAW images).

Because XMP sidecars are used by some other RAW converters, too, you may specify an XMP priority to resolve conflicts between competing recipes. The smaller the number, the higher is the priority.

When using Adobe Lightroom for developing RAW or DNG images, make sure that the option Store changes automatically in XMP is enabled in the Lightroom Catalog Settings. Otherwise, Lightroom would not publish the recipe information.

Adobe Camera Raw and Lightroom usually save this information when you open another image in Camera Raw or Lightroom or when you close the application. If the edited image happens to be located in a watched folder (see Catalog Properties), ZoRa Photo Director will automatically refresh its own catalog thumbnail with the new development recipe.

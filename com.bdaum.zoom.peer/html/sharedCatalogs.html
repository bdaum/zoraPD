Sharing catalogs

If your own node does not define Shared Catalogs, other nodes - even if they specify you as a peer - do not have access to your catalogs and resources. To make catalogs available for other peers, you must explicitely define them in the list of Shared Catalogs.
 See Window>Preferences>Network>Shared Catalogs*

However, sharing catalogs does not only make catalogs accessible to other peers. It allows yourself to search for images across multiple of your own catalogs. If you want to do such a multiple catalog search but do not want to make catalogs accessible to other nodes, simply put them into the list of Shared Catalogs but set their Privacy to "Local".

"Local" is the most severe privacy setting. The other values such as "All", "Medium", or "Only public images" are used to restrict the set of images returned on peer queries according to the Privacy setting in the metadata of each image (Properties>ZoRa Photo Director>Privacy). Local queries are not restricted by this setting.

Finally, the operations performed by other peers can be restricted or extended for each Shared Catalog for each peer computer separately. As a matter of fact this makes only sense for non-local catalogs (the own node always has full access to all catalogs).

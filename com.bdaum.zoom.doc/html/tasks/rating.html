Rating images


Image rating or ranking allows you to classify your images according to their quality. How quality is defined is up to you: technical quality, aesthetical quality, commercial quality, etc. Industry standard are six rating levels from 0 to 5.

Importing rated images

When you import images into a catalog, its rating is imported as well. The rating can be defined within the image file or in an XMP sidecar file.

Rating a single image

The most obvious way to rate a single image is to click on the rating symbol in the top right corner of the images thumbnail image. Another way is to select the image and invoke the Rate... menu or context function.Or drag the image onto a rating collection in the Catalog view. Finally, you may also press a key shortcut, by default Ctrl+Shift+T.
All these actions bring up the rating dialog box where you either click on a rating symbol or press one of the keys 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or * ( * for automated rating).

Rating many images collectivly

Applying the same rank to several images can be done in almost the same way, except the use of the thumbnail rating symbol. Simply select several images, then invoke the Rate... menu or context function or press the rating key shortcut. Or drag the images onto a rating collection in the Catalog view.

Rating many images individually

The most comforable way to do this is to run a slide show on the selected images. As you go press one of the keys 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5. The slide show automatically continues after a short while. Alternatively, you may also move the mouse to bring up the slide show control panel and invoke the rating dialog box from there.

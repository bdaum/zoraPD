Sleeves view

The Sleeves view uses the metaphor of the translucent sleeves used to archive trannies and negatives.

Navigation is quite different from the Lightbox view:
  • There is no scroll bar. Instead you can pan by clicking on the space between slides and dragging the whole sleeve. Ctrl accelerates the panning operation. Depending on which key is reserved for the zoom key (see below), the ALT key or the right mouse button can be used to force panning even when the cursor is positioned on an image. Alternatively you may use the arrow keys for panning.
  • Depending on the settings made under Window>Preferences>Mouse* you can adjust the viewing distance by pressing the zoom key (ALT or Shift) or using the right mouse button and dragging left or right. 
    Alternatively you can zoom in and out with the mouse wheel or use the + and - keys.
  • A double click returns to the initial viewing distance. Alternatively, you can use the * key. Another double click restores the former view.
Also, the slide representation is different:
  • When you select a slide it is brought forward and is enlarged. All other slides are masked with a transparent grey layer.
  • Press the Spacebar to select one slide after the other - a quick and easy mini slide show. Shift+Spacebar works in the opposite direction.
  • You can also press Shift+arrow key to select neighboring slides.

View actions
  • n columns. This will rearrange the slides in a grid of 4 - 24 columns.

For more key commands and mouse gestures please see the general Galleries help page.

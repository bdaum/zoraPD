Configure captions

  • By default, the image title and additional information are shown in image captions. However, the title may completely be switched off or configured using a template.
  • Templates are constructed from constant text parts and variables. You can type in both, but it is much more comfortable and safe to select the variables from a list by clicking the button Insert variable... or Insert metadata.... The later refers to the different fields defined with an image such as EXIF and IPTC data.

    When a variable or metadata cannot be resolved (e.g. a field is undefined) the variable is simply deleted from the template. This may sometimes leave unwanted text fragments within the caption. To avoid this a special syntax is provided:

    Pattern
    		 Description
    (?...?)
    		 Conditional group type 1
    The complete expression between the parantheses will be deleted if all variables and subgroups within the paranthesis remain unresolved.
    Example:
    (?Shot in     {meta=iptc_location_created&city}?)
    (??...?) Conditional group type 2
    The complete expression between the parantheses will be deleted if at least one variable or subgroup within the paranthesis remains unresolved.
    Example:
    (??{meta=owner}, {yyyy}?)
    ...|(...)
    		 Fallback
    If the expression (variable, conditional group, or another fallback) in front of the | is unresolved, the expression within the parantheses is used instead. Otherwise it is discarded,
    Example:
     {meta=iptc_location_created&city}|({title})

    Conditional groups and fallbacks may be nested.
    The mark-up is finally removed from the template when the hover text is generated.
  • In addition, you may specify a fontsize and an alignment but only for custom templates.
